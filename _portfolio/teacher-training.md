## The Challenge

A state education initiative needed to upskill 200+ teachers across 40 schools on competency-based instruction — shifting from textbook delivery to observable skill development in students. Most teachers had 10+ years of experience with traditional methods and limited exposure to learner-centred design.

## My Approach

Resistance to change in experienced educators is real. The programme design had to acknowledge their expertise while building new frameworks alongside it — not replacing one set of instructions with another.

I used the **5E Instructional Model** (Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate) both as the content of the training and as the structure of each session itself — so teachers experienced the model as learners before they were asked to teach with it.

## The Solution

A **3-day residential programme** structured as:

- **Day 1 — Engage & Explore:** Experiential activities that surfaced assumptions about how learning works. Participants taught each other something in 10 minutes using only their existing methods — then reflected on what was and wasn't transferred.
- **Day 2 — Explain & Elaborate:** Frameworks (5E, Bloom's, formative assessment) introduced through case studies from their own subject areas. Small groups redesigned one existing lesson using the new model.
- **Day 3 — Evaluate & Plan:** Peer teaching demos, structured feedback, and 30-day action plans with accountability pairs.

Supporting materials included a **facilitator guide**, **participant workbook**, and a **follow-up resource pack** for use back in school.

## The Outcome

- 94% of participants rated the programme "Very Effective" or "Effective" in post-training survey
- Follow-up at 30 days showed 78% had implemented at least one 5E-structured lesson
- Programme was adopted as a model for the district's ongoing professional development calendar

## What I Learned

Teacher training is uniquely difficult because your learners are themselves skilled educators. They will immediately notice if your facilitation doesn't practise what it preaches. The best design decision I made was to build the entire programme in the model I was teaching — that consistency built credibility faster than any slide deck could.
