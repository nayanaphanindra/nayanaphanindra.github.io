## The Challenge

A global organisation's 3-day in-person induction was expensive, inconsistent across regions, and wasn't translating into confident first-month performance. New hires felt overwhelmed; managers felt underprepared to support them.

## My Approach

I began with a **needs analysis** — interviewing recent joiners, their managers, and L&D stakeholders. The finding: 60% of induction content was "nice to know," not "need to know." Learners were being given information before they had the context to use it.

Using the **ADDIE model** as a backbone, I mapped the 30-day performance journey and worked backwards to identify the critical behaviours for Week 1, Week 2, and Month 1.

## The Solution

A **blended learning path** in three phases:

1. **Pre-joining (async)** — A 20-minute Rise module covering company culture, values, and "what good looks like" in the role.
2. **Day 1 (synchronous)** — A facilitated 3-hour session focused on team introductions, role clarity, and Q&A — not information transfer.
3. **Weeks 2–4 (async + peer)** — Scenario-based Storyline modules with branching decisions, released one per week, tied to real tasks the learner was doing.

## The Outcome

- Time-to-productivity reduced by an estimated **30%** based on manager feedback at 30 days
- Induction delivery cost reduced by **40%** (no travel, facilitator hours halved)
- 94% learner satisfaction in post-programme survey

## What I Learned

The biggest design insight: **timing matters as much as content**. A learner who hasn't started their first project doesn't need a module on stakeholder communication yet. Releasing modules *as work happens* turned learning into a resource, not a task.
